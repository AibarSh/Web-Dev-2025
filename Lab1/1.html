Brief history of the district

Initially, the "Mountain Giant" was called a horticultural collective farm established in the early 1930s with a central estate on the Kamensky Plateau. At the turn of the 40-50s, a new central estate was founded on the collective farm — no longer in the mountains, but on the plain — this was the village of Mountain Giant on the southern outskirts of Alma Ata. From here, they managed at least ten brigades of the horticultural collective farm (later the state farm), located not only in different places of the Kamensky Plateau, but also in the Butakov gorge, around Yunnat Lake (the village of Yubileyny), in different places of Verigina Mountain (Koktobe). In 1982, the Mountain Giant was incorporated into the city limits, and the central estate was transferred to the village of Koktobe. Now it is an entire area located above Al-Farabi Avenue, west of the so-called town of the Ministry of Internal Affairs. From the southeast and east, the Mountain Giant is surrounded by the Yessentai River (formerly. Freckles).
